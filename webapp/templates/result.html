How the TDEE methods differ

  • Mifflin-St Jeor is a widely used estimate for BMR and is a strong default for most people.
  • Harris-Benedict is a classic formula that can give a slightly different result depending on your physical profile.
  • Katch-McArdle uses lean body mass and can be more accurate when body fat is known.
